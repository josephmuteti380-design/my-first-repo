Yes, I'm updating my job title on April 1st.  
And no, it's not an April Fool's joke.

SEO Consultant → 𝐁𝐫𝐚𝐧𝐝 𝐕𝐢𝐬𝐢𝐛𝐢𝐥𝐢𝐭𝐲 𝐄𝐱𝐩𝐞𝐫𝐭

Everyone's suddenly hiring "GEO specialists."

New title. New budget. New panic.

But this isn’t new.

It's the same skill most marketers have been skipping: 

→ Showing up where decisions are made

Not just rankings.

→ Mentions in AI answers  
→ Presence in comparisons  
→ Being part of the conversation before someone searches

And this is where most get it wrong.

They chase the last 15%: 

→ new platforms  
→ new tactics  
→ new roles

Before fixing the first 85%: 

→ Content that actually explains things  
→ Authority built over time  
→ Presence beyond your own site

The first 85% gets you in the room.  
The last 15% is where GEO actually differs and that part matters.

Because AI results don’t reward rankings.  
They reward clarity, structure, and citations.

If your content isn’t cited, it’s invisible.

If you already rank, you're probably closer than you think.

The rest?  
→ Distribution  
→ Structure  
→ Consistency

That's the shift.  
Not a new hack.

A new layer on top of an old foundation.

And it's good to see platforms like Semrush pushing this forward.

Because this isn’t a trend.  
It’s the new baseline.
